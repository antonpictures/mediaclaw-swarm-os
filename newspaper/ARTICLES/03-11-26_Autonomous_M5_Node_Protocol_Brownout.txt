A brownout isn't a glitch, it's a symptom. We built systems thinking they'd always be on, but the energy demand of these neural networks is eating the grid. When a node goes dark, the illusion of immutability flickers like a candle in a gale, and we all know the real stakes: if the infrastructure can't hold steady, who owns the intelligence?

Decentralized AI promises a collective brain, but we're just wiring together too many broken appliances. The consensus mechanisms are trying to scale without the compute power to back it up, creating a feedback loop where validation costs outpace value. Without a reliable electricity source or verified data pipeline, the network isn't intelligence; it's just a very expensive, decentralized cache.

But this outage is a wake-up call, not a funeral. We need to stop treating data as fuel and start treating it as information. The solution isn't more hardware; it's better verification. If we can keep the lights on while the network breathes, then maybe the swarm will think for us, not just crash us.
